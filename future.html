How will the Chinatown Stitch Project change the neighborhood?
The history of Philadelphia's Chinatown is deeply tied to the construction of I-676, whose lasting impacts—including displacement, pollution, traffic issues, and more recent challenges like gentrification and inefficient land use—continue to affect the neighborhood today.

Scenario 0:

Business as Usual

A business-as-usual alternative without the construction of Chinatown Stitch. This alternative is provided here as our reference/control group which will be used to compare results from other scenarios to measure the impacts of the Chinatown Stitch on the local house market.

Scenario 1:

Stitch as a Park

An alternative where the Chinatown Stitch completes its construction and serves as a park and mix-use space as promised. In this scenario, part of the Vine Street Expressway will be capped and transitioned into green open space. With the Chinatown Stitch in place, residents and visitors will have better access to recreational spaces that this neighborhood was previously lacking.

Scenario 2:

(2) More Commercial and Residential Spaces

A reimagined scenario where the Chinatown Stitch creates more commercial opportunities in the area. In this scenario, the Chinatown Stitch not only functions as a park but also creates commercial opportunities along the streets. Capping part of the expressway would also encourage more residents to walk around the area, increasing the demand for commercial services. As the Chinatown Stitch attracts more visitors to the area, these new commercial spaces will expand across the entire neighborhood.

SUMMARY

From Scenario 0 to Scenario 2, the median price rose by more than 30%, indicating a clear market shift—not just in isolated hotspots in the north or south, but across the entire study area. While Scenario 1 helped stabilize the market, Scenario 2 demonstrates that substantial value gains require combining the cap project with broader structural zoning strategies. However, growth remains uneven, and the north side will still require targeted support or policies to fully benefit from future changes.

LIMITATIONS

Note that the purpose of this study is to try to discover the potential trends/shifts in the housing market instead of accurately predicting exact value of each property within the study region due to the limitations of the modeling methods as well as other factors beyond our control. These scenarios are alternatives to reality and not the future market as the future is highly unpredictable.
